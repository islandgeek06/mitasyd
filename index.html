Who we are?

Minority In Technology And Startups. Minority children don’t have access to data, training, information on entrepreneurship, business, salaries and job skills, international job skills.


Our Purpose:

There are historical, institutional and socioeconomical barriers hindering equal access to resources that enable minorities to reach parity in representation in business strategy, data science, technology and the venture eco system. M.I.T.A.S. focuses on racial and socio-economic diversity in these fields. Specifically individuals who:

• Come from underrepresented minority groups, including African American/Black, Hispanic or Latina, Bi/ Multiracial, Native American/Alaskan, Caribbean/West Indian and Native Hawaiian/Pacific Islander

• Come from low-income backgrounds, specifically the families whose median income’s fall below 80 percent of the median income of the region

• Have had lack of exposure or access to data science, business strategy and entrepreneurship

Opportunity for Social Impact

Each Cohort will have 20 participants ages 13-18. Cohorts will begin every six months. Participants will receive a “starter pack” consisting of a laptop computer, headphones, external hard drive, and backpack.

Every Participant will have a mentor on a 1-1 basis. Mentors will connect with mentees on a weekly basis and have a social outing bimonthly. Mentors will assist in homework as they progress through the curriculum, preparation for the pitch competition and general life guidance.

Participants will have access to complimentary counseling sessions by a licensed therapist.

annual pitch competition attended by local businessmen and professional athletes for potential angel investors.

Annual trip to Silicon Valley and meet with underrepresented minorities at large technology firms and stat ups. Visit the campuses of Stanford and Cal Berkley to exposure participants to individuals to various career opportunities and individuals who come from backgrounds that they can identify with.
